# Intended Use and Forbidden Uses

## Intended Use

Vyu is intended to support governed biomedical literature research by helping qualified users search source material, retrieve evidence, generate citation-grounded summaries, inspect evidence quality signals, and produce auditable reports.

The first production pilot should be limited to public or approved licensed literature. Patient-specific PHI/ePHI and treatment recommendations must remain out of scope until privacy, security, regulatory, and clinical safety reviews approve those features.

## Intended Users

- Biomedical researchers.
- Evidence reviewers.
- Clinical policy analysts.
- Internal governance reviewers.

Patient-facing use is not approved for the first production pilot.

## Approved First-Pilot Capabilities

- Literature search and source retrieval.
- Citation-grounded evidence summaries.
- Evidence quality flags and governance warnings.
- Research reports and policy-support drafts.
- Human review queue for high-risk or uncertain outputs.
- Audit export of sources, citations, model settings, and review decisions.

## Forbidden Uses

- Diagnosing, treating, preventing, or managing disease for a specific patient without approved regulatory and clinical safety review.
- Replacing independent professional judgment.
- Producing patient-specific recommendations using PHI/ePHI before PHI handling is approved.
- Hiding source limitations, retraction status, conflicts, uncertainty, or missing evidence.
- Exporting high-risk outputs when required human review has not been completed.
- Using unlicensed source material outside its approved terms.

## Review Gates

Any feature that introduces patient-specific context, automated clinical recommendations, or claims of clinical decision support must pass product, legal/regulatory, privacy, security, and clinical safety review before implementation or customer release.
